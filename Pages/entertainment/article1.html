Investigate officials higher than Zaldy Co

By: Jessica Ann Evangelista




Albie Casiño shared that his becoming a new dad prompted him to call out Slater Young, who is currently in
the public eye for his controversial The Rise at Monterrazas project.


Casiño told the members of the press on Thursday, Nov. 13, in Quezon City during the story conference of his
upcoming film “Andoy” that the reason he has become more political now is because of his son and his current
non-showbiz partner.


“I guess we can thank my baby mama. Kasi parang lagi niyang sinasabi sa akin that you’re so vocal about US politics,
never about your own. Now I have a son, so I guess I’m more political now. Dati naman, wala naman akong pakialam
kung ano mangyayari sa iba. But now that I have a son, I want to leave a better future for him,” he said.


Casiño earlier called out Young on social media for being silent amid renewed backlash over the latter’s
controversial hillside development, which some speculated contributed to the recent flooding in Cebu.


The actor said that although he doesn’t know Young personally, that would not stop him from calling him out
publicly, especially after the celebrity engineer previously claimed that the project would not be “harmful”
to the environment.


“Nung nagsimula ‘yung project, parang maraming siyang sinasabi na hindi daw mangyayari, pero nangyari, so I
just wanna know… And wala masyadong nag-uusap about it, kasi I guess friend nila yung asawa (Kryz Uy), that’s
selective outrage,” he said.


The actor explained that he was “heartbroken” seeing the videos of the flood victims, specifically the clip
where a crying child told his mother that they have lost everything while they were on the roof of their house
because of the floodwaters.


“It’s just sad the state of what’s going on now. Like I said, before ‘yung talagang tingin ko sa buhay is wala
naman akong pakialam kung ano mangyayari sa politics kasi hindi naman mag-iiba ‘yung buhay ko doon,” he said.


“But I guess when I had a son, I became more empathetic to other people. Yung mga videos lang na lumalabas,
like the video of them on the roof, tas yung bata, sabi, Mommy, we lost everything, diba, parang… It breaks your
heart,” he continued.


Casiño further urged Young to speak up and address the backlash, stating, “As a man, maybe speak up, diba?
Say something. Condolences to the families, to the people who lost loved ones. Kahit ‘yun na lang.”


The 32-year-old actor, who also previously called out Zaldy Co in relation to the anomalous flood control projects
and the rampant corruption in the Philippines, said that his Christmas wish is for corrupt individuals to be finally
held accountable.


“I don’t wish death on anybody because there’s no coming back from death. Pero let’s hold them accountable.
Doon na lang,” he said.
